The aesthetics of this assignment were inspired by a disco ball. I initially started with dynamically moving bricks. Afterwards, I was playing around with mouse tracking, and got inspired by the idea of creating a spotlight. Then, I combined this with the video so that whatever the user moved their mouse over, they could have a little keyed out frame of their video feedback. Then, I wanted to have this little “spotlight” feel a bit different from the rest of the foreground, so I added some flashing colors. Afterwards, I wanted to add some effects to the video, and played around with feedback until there were repeating circles of the user's video. Combined with the initial “bricks,” I had a vaguely disco feel for the foreground, and feedback in the “spotlight” that mirrored the foreground with changing lights and mini “bricks”.

Overall, I wanted to explore combining two separate design ideas into one. And, I wanted to have things like video and feedback affect one another. I also spent a while trying to figure out how to use time to affect the output in the same way as frame. Lastly, I wanted to explore the capabilities of TweakPane.
